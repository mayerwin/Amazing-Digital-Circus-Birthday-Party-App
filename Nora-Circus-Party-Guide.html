🌀 Teleport ritual — split in two so YOU control the "ZAP!"
The teleport brackets the walk between stations, in two beats:
  1. Leaving a station — kids put goggles DOWN and follow you. Optionally play 🌀 Teleport — goggles down so Caine says it: "Goggles down, into the swirly portal, follow Bubble…"

The "3-2-1 ZAP!" lives on the console's next station step, so it waits for your tap — never auto-fires before you're there. Keeps the eye-masks useful all adventure (goggles down only for the short walk) and gives stragglers a job: carry their Gloink like a relay baton.
The idea in one line
Caine the ringmaster hides 4 magic Gloinks around the circus. Kids complete 4 silly challenges to find them, pass a quick Human Test, then crack the Exit Code on the iPad to escape — and hunt down the hidden treasure, before Caine calls cake time. 🎂
⭐ How you'll run it: the Caine Console + you as Bubble
The best way: open the Caine Console on the iPad (the caine-console app). You hold the iPad and play Bubble, Caine's helper: you press NEXT at each step, run the little games, and lead the Gloink hunts. The kids hear Caine; you're his sidekick guiding them.
Prefer to do it without the tech? Every line below is also written out — just voice Caine yourself. The scripts below ARE the console's words, so nothing's lost either way. (See the Prep tab to set up the console + voice.)

What you need staged in advance

  • An eye mask per kid (the "VR headset").

  • The "Exit Door" = the upstairs guest toilet door — decorate it with the printable EXIT sign. (They get teleported UP to it for the finale.) Nothing needs to be behind it.
  • A "treasure chest" (box/basket) of candy bags, hidden somewhere near the top of the stairs for the post-escape treasure hunt. The cake is set up separately (upstairs too — the kitchen/living room at road level), not behind the door.
  • The iPad with the Caine Console open (the caine-console app), volume up. You carry it as Bubble.
  • Printable station signs + the Exit Door sign from the Print Pack.

📱 iPad — the Caine Console (talking)

The Caine Console is the star: it walks through every adventure step and speaks each one in Caine's voice (English, short pause, then French — in OmniVoice), while it follows your 📡 Adventure remote (Adventure tab). The exit-code screen is built in near the end. Start it by double-clicking Start Caine Party.bat in the party folder, then on Nora's iPad open the address it prints and tap 🎪 Caine's Console.

On Nora's iPad, tap the big "▶ TAP TO START" once when the console opens — Safari needs one tap before it will play sound. After that every step auto-plays (and your remote here drives it). No audio made yet? It still works — it shows the words to read.

🎤 "Talk to Caine" — let kids speak to him live

Near the top of the console are two buttons: 🎤 Talk to Caine and 🎤 Parler à Caine. A child taps one, talks ("Caine, where's my Gloink?"), taps Stop & Send — Caine thinks for a couple of seconds and answers out loud, in his own voice, staying in character and remembering the whole conversation. It works in English and French.

One-time setup (needed for the microphone): iPads only allow the mic over a secure (https) address. When the server starts it prints a line like
https://192.168.1.x:8766/console — open that address on Nora's iPad (not the plain http one), and the first time tap through Safari's "Not Private" warning (Show details → visit this website). After that the mic buttons work. The plain http address is fine for everything else.

On the laptop: the console uses Claude (your subscription) to write Caine's replies and your warmed-up OmniVoice to speak them — no per-use cost. If a reply ever can't be voiced it still shows Caine's words on screen. If the buttons say the mic needs the https address, you opened the plain http one — switch to the https link above.

🎙️ Make Caine's voice say their names

One script does it all. Put a short clean Caine clip named caine_ref.wav in the caine-voice folder, then run python make_caine_voice.py. It generates every line with the kids' names baked in — a personality intro for each one ("Chloé, you are RAGATHA, the kindest ragdoll!"), a different kid named to carry each Gloink, and "Happy birthday, Nora!" — into caine-console/audio/.

Languages: general lines + Nora & Leo get English then French (short pause between); the other kids get French only. The chosen voice is OmniVoice (best, native French); F5 & IndexTTS-2 are also available to compare/tune in the 🎛 Voice Studio.

Both attendance scenarios are baked in — audio for Nina & Max is generated too, so you just flip present:true/false in the console's ROSTER on the day. No re-running. No Python? Use the Colab notebook. Full steps + model notes in caine-voice/README.md.

Worth doing a few days early — it's the bit the kids will talk about. Optional: skip it and read Caine's lines yourself, or record your own voice as the reference clip.
